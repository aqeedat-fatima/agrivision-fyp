AI-powered disease detection

Computer vision on crop leaf images for fast, accessible disease screening.

AgriVision analyzes uploaded leaf images using a trained deep learning model to identify common crop diseases (with a focus on cotton). The system returns a predicted class along with confidence, helping users understand how certain the model is.

To improve real-world reliability, we also perform photo quality checks (blur + brightness) before presenting results, reducing the chance of misleading predictions from unclear images.

User-friendly dashboard & monitoring history

A dashboard that doesn’t just show a label—it supports monitoring over time.

The dashboard organizes scans, predictions, and risk levels in a clean workflow so farmers and experts can review results quickly. We also maintain history tracking so repeated scans can be compared across days and fields.

This is important because monitoring is not a one-time action—farmers need continuity, trend visibility, and a simple interface that makes decision-making easier.

Reports & PDF summaries

Clear outputs for documentation, field visits, and expert review.

AgriVision generates structured summaries including prediction results, confidence, and basic risk interpretation. Visual analytics help users identify patterns in disease occurrence and model certainty over time.

PDF reporting is included to make it easy to share results with agronomists, supervisors, and stakeholders without requiring access to the full dashboard.

Growth-stage & satellite monitoring

Turning “disease detection” into real field monitoring.

Based on feedback, AgriVision is being extended to support crop growth-stage monitoring, because farmers want to track field progress—not only diagnose disease from a single image.

We also integrate satellite-based vegetation indices (NDVI/EVI) to detect stress trends at the field level, enabling earlier alerts and better decision support.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does AgriVision detect crop diseases?

AgriVision uses an AI-powered image classification model (EfficientNet-B3). Farmers upload a crop or leaf image, and the system detects diseases with confidence scores and visual Grad-CAM explanations.

Does AgriVision support satellite monitoring?

Yes. The platform integrates Sentinel-2 satellite data to calculate vegetation indices like NDVI, EVI, and NDWI. This helps monitor field health, water stress, and crop trends over time.

Is the platform bilingual?

Absolutely. AgriVision provides both Urdu and English support to ensure accessibility for local farmers across Pakistan. The platform is designed with simple visuals, clear alerts, and easy navigation so farmers can use it confidently without technical expertise.

Who can use AgriVision?

The platform is designed for farmers, agricultural researchers, and policymakers who want simple, visual, and data-driven crop health insights.
